Population of England, Wales, Scotland and N.Ireland

Bar Chart of Populations of the UK

Sometimes the records show England and Wales, sometimes records show England, Wales separatly. Most of the records before the later Census's on population from 1801 will show the counties indivdually anything before 1801 will show the countries as England and Wales. N.Ireland did not officially become part of the UK until 1922, therefore many census's before then would not show the seperate populations from Ireland and therefore may not appear on census's for the UK before then. There are little estimates of Scottish population over the ages, we know from Roman estimates and also the Catholic Priests in Scotland of some very early population numbers. Because of the Norman efficency documents were readly produced for nearly all aspects of life like the Romans. This included population estimates. The Black Death caused a huge decrease in population numbers, not just in the UK but also in Europe. Couple that with much migration away from the UK over many periods in History the numbers changed drastically, making documentation harder as there was obviously no formal way of tracking peoples movements.
